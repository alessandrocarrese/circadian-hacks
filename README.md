# Light & Health: The Science of Spectral Exposure

Research-based articles on how artificial lighting affects human health, metabolism, and circadian biology.

## Overview

Modern LED lighting differs dramatically from natural sunlight in ways that standard metrics like color temperature (CCT) fail to capture. This research explores the spectral differences and their biological implications, based on original measurements of 91+ light sources.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Natural Baseline |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| SPR (Spectral Protection Ratio) | Red+NIR / Blue balance | 1.74+ (shade) |
| S/M Ratio | S-cone / Melanopsin stimulation | 0.69-1.13 |
| NIR % | Near-infrared content | 17-22% |
| Melanopic EDI | Circadian stimulus | Varies by time |

## Data Source

All analysis based on original spectroradiometer measurements of:
- 33+ natural light conditions (dawn to dusk, sun to shade)
- 24 LED bulbs across brands and color temperatures
- 5 window/filter types
- Computer monitors and displays
